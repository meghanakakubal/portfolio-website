Figure Skating: Data Breakdown

In this final project for my Data Journalism course, I completed a thorough analysis of the scoring system used by professional figure skaters. Discovering and comparing the scoring trends of male and female competitors led to realizations about the potential harms of the current practices in figure skating. I also utilized "Scrollytelling" for the first time, implementing Scrollama.js, Photoshop, HTML, and CSS skills successfully.

Data Journalism Research Article

In this article I wrote about the intersection of the data science and journalism fields. After conducting research, interviewing professionals, and several rounds of drafting, we published this article in the NYU Data Science Club's publication. Through this process, I gained understanding of the tools (such as Flourish, Idyll, JavaScript, and d3) and resources needed for practicing Data Journalism. The article has had more than 700 views and more than 90 'claps'.

Music & Mental Health Article

Music has been a part of my life since I was five years old, and it's difficult to overstate how impactuful the art has been on me. Motivated by my perosonal relationship with music, I delved into the connections between music and mental health with my final project for my written journalism course. I researched COVID-19 induced mental health trends, the efficacy of music therapy, and interviewed popular YouTuber Guitaro5000 and NYU Steinhardt professor Ken Aigen.

Special Education in a Pandemic

Soon after schools shut down in the spring of 2020 in response to COVID-19, issues arose regarding the equity of online education for special needs students. I covered this story for my local NPR station, KUOW. After researching, interviewing, producing, recording, and editing, the piece was aired and published.
